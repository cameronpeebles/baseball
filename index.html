Welcome to Bluebonnet Baseball Analytics

This site is a full analytics platform for the Bluebonnet Baseball Federation - a 10-team rotisserie league scoring 10 categories over 20 weekly periods. Every page connects to live Fantrax data fetched automatically by a GitHub Action.

2Match Predictions - Check win probability vs your current opponent. Which categories are you likely to lose?
3SGP → By Position - Is there a free agent or trade target who upgrades your weakest slot?
4Trade Waiver Wire Targets - Any underperforming hitters or pitchers worth picking up?
5JOE Factor - Are you running lucky or unlucky? Adjust expectations accordingly.

Dashboard overview
There are two dashboards. The Dashboard tab shows your team's live stats, matchup, JOE factor luck, positional strength, top fWAR players, trade suggestions, and waiver targets. The New Dashboard tab shows a roster health view with drop candidates, add targets, and positional strength vs the league. Both update when you change team from the dropdown. Use Refresh All Data at the top of either dashboard to run all data pipelines at once — this ensures every chart and table is populated before you navigate to other pages.
Roster overview — how to read the charts

The Roster Overview card appears on both dashboards. It contains five panels: two donuts, two radars, and a scatter plot.

The two donut charts

The left donut shows fWAR by group — how your total fantasy value is distributed across Infield, Outfield, SP, and RP. A large IF+OF slice means you are a hitting-driven team. A large SP slice means your pitchers are carrying the team.

The right donut shows positional strength by group — the average player rating of your rostered players in each group as a percentile. This reflects depth and quality, not just total output. A balanced donut means even depth across all four groups.

Key insight: compare the two donuts. If fWAR is hitter-heavy but positional strength is balanced, your pitchers have good ratings but low fantasy output — they are underperforming. If fWAR is pitcher-heavy but positional strength shows weak pitching, a few elite arms are masking thin depth behind them.

Example: hitter-heavy fWAR, balanced positional strength
fWAR by group
IF +38, OF +31, SP +5, RP +4
IF +38 OF +31 SP +5 RP +4
Positional strength by group
IF 52, OF 54, SP 49, RP 47
IF 52 OF 54 SP 49 RP 47

The fWAR donut is dominated by IF+OF (88% of value from hitters) while the positional strength donut is nearly equal quarters. Pitchers have decent ratings but very low fantasy output. Expect to compete in hitting categories and struggle in ERA, WHIP, K, W, SVH3.

The radar charts

Each radar has two lines. The solid line (navy for hitting, green for pitching) shows positional strength — your average player rating percentile in each category. The dashed red line shows fWAR% — how your players are actually performing relative to all rostered players at their position.

Larger always means better — including for ERA and WHIP. A high ERA percentile means your ERA is low (good), because the z-score conversion inverts these stats before plotting.

The gap between the two lines is the key signal. When fWAR% is inside positional strength, players are underperforming their ratings. When fWAR% bulges outside, players are overperforming.

Example: underperforming (fWAR% inside)
Pos strength large, fWAR% small inside — pitchers underperforming ratings.
Pos Strength fWAR%

Well-rated pitchers not delivering. Sell high on reputation — regression risk or injury candidates.

Example: overperforming (fWAR% outside)
fWAR% bulging outside pos strength — pitchers overperforming their ratings.
Pos Strength fWAR%

Lower-rated pitchers outperforming expectations. Could be a breakout or luck-driven — cross-check with JOE Factor before trading.

JOE Factor - Scheduling Luck

JOE Factor answers: "If you had played every other team each week, what would your average win% have been - and how does your actual record compare?"

How It's Calculated
Step 1 - Pairwise H2H: For each team each week, simulate a head-to-head matchup vs every other team. Compare all 10 categories. ERA/WHIP are lower-is-better.
Step 2 - Avg Case W%: Average your win% if you'd played all 9 opponents. This is your "deserved" performance.
Step 3 - Luck z-score: luck_z = (Actual W% − Avg Case W%) / std(differences across all teams that week)
Step 4 - JOE Factor: Min-max normalize luck z-scores to [−1, +1] across all teams. +1 = luckiest, −1 = unluckiest.
Charts
Whisker Plot - Each team's vertical range from Worst to Best case. Dots: dark green = Best, gold = Avg, green = Actual, red = Worst.
JOE Bar Chart - Green bars = lucky, red bars = unlucky.
H2H Score Matrix - What the score would have been between every pair of teams. ★ = your actual opponent that week.
Season Avg tab - Averages all metrics across every completed week. This is the source of the Dashboard luck tile.
Player Data - Metric Reference

The Player Data page surfaces four key metrics for every player. Here's what each one means:

Metric What It Measures
League Rating How a player's overall performance stacks up against all players at their type (all hitters or all pitchers), adjusted for sample size.
Pos Rating How strong a player is relative to others at their specific position, highlighting positional advantage or scarcity.
fWAR The total value a player has produced this season above a replacement-level option, based on your league's scoring categories.
fWAR % The player's percentile rank in fWAR within their pool (all hitters, all SP, or all RP), showing how their production compares to peers in the league.
fWAR - Fantasy Wins Above Replacement

fWAR estimates how many fantasy wins a player contributes above a freely available replacement-level player at their position. Higher = more valuable. Configure the model on the Models page.

fWAR Calculation Flow
Step 1 - Z-Score each stat
For every rostered player, each scoring category is z-scored against all rostered players in their broad position group (Infield, Outfield, SP, RP).
z = (player_stat − group_mean) / group_std
ERA and WHIP are negated so lower = better.
Step 2 - Weighted Total Z
Z-scores are summed across all relevant categories, each scaled by its category weight (0–1, adjustable in the Models page).
totalZ = Σ (z_cat × weight_cat)
Step 3 - Playing Time Weight
Total Z is scaled by how much a player has played relative to the average rostered player at their role - capped at 1.0 (never penalises players for playing too much).
ptWeight = min(1.0, playerIP / avgRosteredIP)
Hitters use AB. Pitchers use IP - computed separately for SP and RP so closers are compared to other closers, not penalised by starters who accumulate IP much faster. A closer at 10 IP is compared against the average rostered RP's IP total, not the SP average.
Step 4 - Replacement Level
The Nth-ranked player at each position defines replacement level - the baseline of a freely available player. N is the positional replacement rank (configurable in Models). A lower N = harder position to fill = higher bar.
Step 5 - fWAR
fWAR = playerTotalZ − replacementLevelZ
Positive = above replacement. Negative = below replacement. A +2.0 fWAR player contributes roughly 2 wins above what you'd get by picking up a free agent.
Positional Scarcity Presets
⚾ Pitcher Heavy - SP:147, RP:63. Values pitching depth.
⚖️ Hybrid (default) - SP:123, RP:53, OF:85. Balanced.
🏏 Hitter Heavy - SP:98, OF:100. Values hitting depth.
🎯 Optimal - Weights derived from a scipy optimizer maximizing Spearman rank correlation between team fWAR and points standings across 50 historical team-seasons (2021–2025). Spearman r is shown on the button.
Important: fWAR uses cumulative season stats
fWAR reflects how a player has performed so far this season relative to their peers - it is not a projection. Use the SGP Engine (Player Trade & Market) for forward-looking player value.
fWAR % - Percentile Column

The fWAR % column on the Player Data page converts a player's raw fWAR into a 0–100 percentile rank within their pool of rostered players. It answers: "How does this player's fWAR compare to everyone else currently rostered?"

Hitters
All rostered IF + OF in one pool
SP
All rostered SP in one pool
RP
All rostered RP in one pool
Why separate SP and RP? SPs and RPs have fundamentally different workloads and fWAR scales. A closer with 15 saves and a 2.50 ERA shouldn't be compared against a SP with 180 IP - they're contributing in different ways.
Why rostered only? The percentile measures your standing among active competition - the players other managers are actually deploying. FA players vary wildly in quality and would dilute the signal.
How to use it: A hitter in the 80th percentile is more valuable than 80% of all rostered hitters by fWAR. Sort by fWAR % descending to quickly identify the most impactful players in the league - useful for trade targets and waiver wire decisions.
Player Ratings - Pos Rating & League Rating

The Player Data page shows two rating columns alongside fWAR. Both are 0–100 percentile scores but measure different things.

POS RATING
How good is this player at their specific position?

A catcher is rated only against other catchers. A shortstop only against shortstops. Multi-position players take their best rating across all eligible positions.
LEAGUE RATING
How good is this player vs all hitters or all pitchers?

All hitters compete in one pool. SP in one pool, RP in another. A 90 League Rating means the player outperforms 90% of all rostered players at their type.
Rating Calculation Flow
Step 1 - Build position pools
All rostered players are indexed by fine position: C, 1B, 2B, 3B, SS, OF, SP, RP. Multi-position players appear in multiple pools.
Step 2 - Percentile each category with sample-size weighting
For each scoring category, the player's percentile rank within their pool is computed. Rate stats (AVG, ERA, WHIP) are downweighted based on sample size:
Hitters (AB)
0–49 → 0.2×
50–99 → 0.4×
100–149 → 0.6×
150+ → 1.0×
SP (IP)
0–9 → 0.2×
10–19 → 0.4×
20–34 → 0.6×
35–49 → 0.8×
50+ → 1.0×
RP (IP): 0–4 → 0.2× · 5–9 → 0.4× · 10–14 → 0.6× · 15–24 → 0.8× · 25+ → 1.0×
Step 3 - Weighted average → 0–100 score
rating = Σ(percentile_cat × factor_cat) / Σ(factor_cat)
Multi-pos players keep the best rating across all eligible positions as their Pos Rating.

Player Profile Radar ChartHow the three lines are calculated

When you click a player row on the Player Data page, a radar chart appears showing three overlapping lines across the five scoring categories. All three lines use the same 0–100 scale where 50 = league average, so they can be directly compared.

fWAR% (Yellow)
Per-category z-score percentile vs all rostered players in the same pool (all hitters, SP, or RP). Incorporates fWAR model weights - categories your league values more score higher.
Pos Rating (Mint)
Per-category raw stat percentile vs position peers only (e.g. SS vs SS). Sample-size weighted. Shows where this player excels or lags relative to others at their position.
League Rating (Blue)
Per-category raw stat percentile vs all rostered hitters or pitchers. Sample-size weighted. Shows the player's absolute strength in each category across the whole league.
Calculation Steps
fWAR% line - z-score percentile
For each category, the player's fw.z[cat] is ranked against all rostered players in the same pool. below / total → 0–1 → × 100. ERA and WHIP z-scores are already negated in the fWAR model (lower ERA = higher z-score), so all categories are treated as higher = better - no additional inversion needed.
Pos Rating & League Rating lines - stat percentile
For each category, the raw stat is percentile-ranked within the relevant pool. The raw percentile is centred: dev = (pct − 0.5) × sampleFactor, then re-centred at 0.5 for display: v = dev + 0.5 → × 100.
Key difference between the three lines
fWAR% uses z-scores (which bake in your league's category weights), while Pos Rating and League Rating use raw stats. A player might score high in fWAR% for SB because SB is scarce, even if their raw SB total looks modest - this divergence is intentional and signals fantasy-specific value.
Reading the chart: When fWAR% (yellow) sits above League Rating (blue) in a category, the player is generating more fantasy value in that category than their raw stats suggest - often due to category scarcity or weight. When it sits below, the raw stat is strong but not converting to fantasy value.

SGP measures how many standings positions a player's rest-of-season stats would gain your team. It's personalized to your current weaknesses.

Formulas
Counting: SGP = ROS_stat / denominator

ERA: SGP = (teamERA − playerERA) × ipShare / 0.106
Category Weighting

SGP is multiplied by a weight based on your current standings rank in that category:

weight = 0.2 + (rank − 1) / (n − 1) × 2.3

Rank 1 = 0.2× (you're winning this, don't need more). Rank 10 = 2.5× (you desperately need improvement here).

SGP - Roster ImpactHow would adding a player change your standings?

The SGP page simulates adding any rostered player's rest-of-season projections to a selected team and shows exactly which categories improve - and by how many standings positions.

How it works
Step 1 - Current standings
Each team's cumulative season stats are pulled from standings_season.json. All 10 teams are ranked 1–10 in each category. ERA and WHIP are ranked inversely (lower = better = rank 1).
Step 2 - ROS projections
FantasyPros consensus rest-of-season projections are loaded from fantasypros_hitters.json and fantasypros_pitchers.json. Only rostered players with a matching FP projection appear.
Step 3 - Simulate
The player's ROS projected stats are added to the selected team's current totals. Counting stats (R, HR, RBI, SB, K, W, SVH3) are added directly. Rate stats are volume-weighted:
AVG = (current_hits + ros_hits) / (current_AB + ros_AB) ERA = (current_ER + ros_ER) / total_IP × 9 WHIP = (current_baserunners + ros_baserunners) / total_IP
Step 4 - Rerank & display
All 10 teams are re-ranked with the new totals. Each category shows a grey bar (current rank), a green/red bar (projected rank), the rank labels (e.g. 6→1), and a delta badge (e.g. ▲5). A longer bar means a better rank. Green = improvement, red = decline, grey = no change.
Select a team - simulates adding players to that specific team's current stat totals. Changing teams re-runs all simulations instantly.
Select a position - filters the player pool to that position. Hitting positions (C, 1B, 2B, 3B, SS, OF) show the 5 hitting categories; pitching positions (SP, RP) show the 5 pitching categories.
Note on SV vs SVH3: FantasyPros projects saves (SV) but your league scores saves + holds (SVH3). SV is used as a proxy - RP projections will understate SVH3 value for high-hold relievers.
Trade Waiver Wire Targets

Identifies hitters and pitchers whose underlying Statcast metrics suggest their stats will improve (buy low) or decline (sell high).

Hitter Signals
🟢 Underperforming (Buy Low): Δ xwOBA ≥ +0.020 AND Δ BABIP ≤ −0.020 AND Δ Barrel% ≥ 0. Hitting the ball harder than stats show - expect improvement.
🔴 Overperforming (Sell High): Δ xwOBA ≤ −0.020 AND Δ BABIP ≥ +0.020 AND Δ Barrel% ≤ 0. Stats inflated by luck - expect regression.
Grade (Δ Hard Hit%): ≥ +2% = Buy Low · ≥ +3% = Good Buy Low · ≥ +4% = Elite Buy Low
Pitcher Signals
🟢 Underperforming: Δ(xERA − ERA) ≥ +0.50 AND Δ BABIP ≥ +0.020 AND Δ K% ≥ 0. ERA is worse than expected - strikeout rate holding.
Grade (Δ Fastball Velo): ≥ +0.5 mph = Buy Low · ≥ +0.75 mph = Good · ≥ +1.0 mph = Elite
Quadrant Chart

X-axis = Δ xwOBA−wOBA · Y-axis = fWAR. Circles = rostered, squares = FA. Dropdown to color by quadrant, fantasy team, Hard Hit%, Barrel%, or their deltas. Hover any dot for player details.

Match Predictions

Predicts every period matchup using a blend of head-to-head category analysis and season standings points.

Category H2H (60% weight)

Compares each team's cumulative average stats across all prior periods. The team winning more of the 10 categories is predicted to win. ERA/WHIP are lower-is-better.

catProb = away_cat_wins / 10
Points Strength (40% weight)

Uses cumulative season fantasy points as a second signal. A sigmoid function converts the points difference into win probability.

diff = (teamA_pts − teamB_pts) / pts_range
ptsProb = 1 / (1 + e^(−6 × diff))
winProb = 0.60 × catProb + 0.40 × ptsProb

Winner if prob > 0.52. Tie if within 0.48–0.52 band.

Correlations

Shows how strongly each category correlates with fantasy points standings - tells you what's actually driven winning in this league.

Two Tabs
2026 Season - 10 data points (one per team). Quick signal but noisy at this sample size.
Historical 2021-2025 - 50 data points (10 teams × 5 seasons). Much more reliable. Use this to guide your roster strategy.
Pearson r Interpretation
|r| ≥ 0.7 - Strong. This category has consistently driven standings. Chase it.
|r| 0.4–0.7 - Moderate. Meaningful but not decisive.
|r| < 0.2 - Weak. Deprioritize. Winning this category hasn't historically translated to standings.

ERA and WHIP signs are inverted (lower = better) so positive r means better ERA = more points.

Glossary
fWAR - Fantasy Wins Above Replacement. How many fantasy wins a player contributes above a freely available replacement at their position.
SGP - Standings Gain Points. How many standings positions a player's projected stats would gain your team. Based on the average gap between adjacent teams in each category.
JOE Factor - Scheduling luck metric. Compares your actual win% to the average win% you'd have gotten if you'd played every team. Normalized to [−1, +1].
xwOBA - Expected weighted on-base average. Measures quality of contact (exit velocity + launch angle) to estimate what a hitter's wOBA "should" be.
wOBA - Weighted on-base average. Like OBP but weights each outcome (walk, single, HR etc.) by its actual run value.
xERA - Expected ERA. What a pitcher's ERA "should" be based on quality of contact allowed, independent of defense and luck.
BABIP - Batting Average on Balls In Play. A high BABIP often indicates luck; a low BABIP can signal bad luck. Typical range: .280–.320.
Barrel% - Percentage of batted balls hit with ideal exit velocity (95+ mph) and launch angle (26–30°). Strong predictor of future HR and xwOBA.
Hard Hit% - Percentage of batted balls hit 95+ mph. General measure of contact quality.
Z-Score - How far above or below average a value is, measured in standard deviations. Z = (value − mean) / std. Used throughout the site for cross-category comparisons.
Pearson r - Correlation coefficient from −1 to +1. Measures how linearly related two variables are. |r| ≥ 0.7 = strong, 0.4–0.7 = moderate, < 0.2 = weak.
Spearman r - Like Pearson r but based on rank order rather than raw values. More robust to outliers. Used in the fWAR optimizer.
Replacement Level - The performance of a freely available player (waiver wire / end of bench). fWAR measures value above this baseline, not above zero.
SVH3 - Saves + Holds × 3-point value. Your league's combined saves/holds scoring category.
ROS - Rest of season. Projections scaled by current pace: ROS = current_stat × (remaining_games / games_played).
